Unit 3: Final Project and Presentations

Overview

The goal of this project is for you to apply panel data analysis that we have covered in the course to answer a real world question. This is also a chance to showcase your creativity and analytic skills. Create something that you are proud to show others.

Rubric

The Question

The question Choose one of the treatment or exposure datasets and find an outcome to evaluate. You are posing some form of the question: What is the impact of \(x\) (treatment or exposure) on some outcome \(y\)?

Treatment or exposure datasets

The Data

You are given the treatment or exposure dataset. You are responsible for collecting additional data needed to answer this question. First, you need to identify data on the outcome \(y\). Second, you need to identify data on any controls that you may need (e.g., weather). Including control variables in the regression explains a portion of the variation in your data leaving your variable of interest to explain the remaining variation. You are responsible for merging or joining your datasets. This means that you must identify the common unit of analysis in each of your datasets and aggregate data if necessary.

Additional Datasets

The Analysis

We expect you to use fixed-effects regression to answer your proposed question. The only strict requirements are:

  1. Present summary statistics of your data. Many of the tabular or data visualization techniques covered in previous units apply to panel data. You should make clear the number of units and time periods in your dataset.

  2. Perform a fixed-effects regression.

  3. Explain your analysis. Is your analysis designed to estimate a treatment effect (of a policy that is present or not) or the marginal effect of an exposure (a continuous variable). You should clearly explain how the analysis helps to answer your research question.

  4. Discuss any assumptions you made in your analysis and identify limitations.

  5. Present and interpret your results. Use your analysis to clearly answer your research question and tell the audience what to do with the information provided. Provide interpretations of regression results (treatment effect or marginal effect). Use appropriate visualizations to communicate results.

Overall presentation expectations: (20 points total)

Your presentations should be 6-9 minutes long. (5 points)

Presentation Data and Analysis: (35 points total)

Data:

  • Describe where your data is from, any modifications to the original data, and how you combined disparate data into a single dataset (10 points)

  • Provide summary statistics and visualizations (5 points, see step 1 under Analysis above)

Analysis:

  • What assumptions did you make in your analysis? What are limitations to your analysis? (10 points, see step 3 under Analysis above)

Presentation Discussion/Conclusion: (10 points total)

  • How did you use your analysis to answer your question? What is the answer to your question?
